Venue

Buchsenhausen Castle, nestled in the picturesque city of Innsbruck, Austria, stands as a captivating testament to history and architectural splendor. Dating back to the 16th century, this Renaissance-style castle exudes timeless charm and cultural significance.

Today, Buchsenhausen Castle serves as a cultural and artistic hub. It houses the internationally renowned International Fellowship Program for Art and Theory, which invites artists and theorists from around the globe to engage in interdisciplinary dialogue and creative exploration. The castle's tranquil surroundings, including its beautiful gardens, provide an inspiring backdrop for the exchange of ideas and the pursuit of artistic endeavors.
